How does big data relate to economics?

To understand how these two topics relate, you must first understand what economics is. If you have ever wondered why some countries are rich and some are poor or what causes recessions, this is why we have economics. Economics is a social science that involves the production, distribution and consumption of goods and services. It focuses on how individuals, businesses and governments make choices with the allocation of resources to satisfy wants and needs.


Data is becoming larger than ever and this provides a new opportunity for economists to predict things more accurately than ever before. These large data sets allow for more flexible relationships than the traditional linear models. Machine learning techniques like decision trees, support vector machines, neural nets and deep learning are used to create these relationships.


One of the hardest parts about economics is the ability to find correlation and causation. In the past, there weren't large sample sizes to work with to create the economics statistics that are accurate enough to find correlation. The gathering of this data now makes this possible. Economists are now using this data in their research to make predictions and create new laws in economics.


To what extent should the government intervene with the market?

What are the signs that a recession may occur soon?

What kind of affects does government spending have on the economy?


These are just a few questions that economists are trying to accurately answer. With the help of big data, there will be more definitive answers to these questions in the future.
